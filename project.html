Projects

Throughout my coursework, I have worked on several projects involving statistical analysis, predictive modeling, and data visualization.

Crime Premise Prediction Using Logistic Regression
In this project, we analyzed crime data to study whether the type of location where a crime occurs (residential vs. commercial) could be predicted using victim demographics. We built a logistic regression model using variables such as victim age, sex, and descent. The project involved data cleaning, model fitting, interpreting coefficients, and visualizing results.

Statistical Poster Presentation
As part of a research presentation, our team designed a data science poster using the Better Poster format. The poster summarized our research question, statistical methods, model results, and conclusions in a clear visual layout.

Model Selection Teaching Video
For another project, our group created a short instructional video explaining how to perform model selection in data science. The video explained steps such as preparing data, splitting datasets into training and validation sets, comparing candidate models, and selecting the best model based on evaluation metrics.

Data Visualization Projects
I have also created visualizations in R to explore relationships between variables, including bar plots, scatterplots, and statistical summaries. These visualizations help communicate patterns in data and support statistical conclusions.

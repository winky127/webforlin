About Me

My name is Yinglin Wu and I am currently a Statistics and Data Science student at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA). I am interested in how statistical methods and data analysis can be used to better understand real-world problems and support decision making.

My academic interests include statistical modeling, data analysis, and data visualization. I enjoy working with data to identify patterns, build predictive models, and communicate results clearly through graphs, reports, and presentations.

Through my coursework and projects, I have gained experience using tools such as R and Python for data analysis. I am particularly interested in combining analytical work with clear visual communication, so that complex statistical results can be understood by a broader audience.

Outside of technical work, I also enjoy building websites and designing digital presentations that present data in a clear and engaging way.
